🔧 The TPM Toolkit

OKR (Objectives & Key Results)

Objective: Qualitative goal (e.g., "Improve refund speed")
Key Results: Measurable outcomes (e.g., "Reduce refund processing time from 3 days to 1 day")

📅 Launch Checklist

  • ☑️ Engineering: Code complete, tested, reviewed
  • ☑️ Observability: Dashboards, alerts, logs ready
  • ☑️ Support: Trained, runbooks ready, escalation paths clear
  • ☑️ Docs: API docs, customer-facing guides written
  • ☑️ Communications: Announcement draft, stakeholder alignment
  • ☑️ Rollback: Tested, team knows when/how to rollback

💬 Interview Q&A

Q1: A feature is slipping 3 weeks. What do you do?

A: Escalate immediately. Understand the root cause (scope creep? technical difficulty? resource constrained?). Discuss options with team: cut scope, add resources, or rescope timeline. Communicate impact to stakeholders ASAP, don't wait until last day.

Q2: Two teams disagree on priority. How do you resolve?

A: Align on OKRs and business outcomes, not opinions. If both teams' work supports the same OKR, sequence them with clear dependencies. If conflicting OKRs, escalate to leadership for priority decision.

Q3: You discover a risk the day before launch. What's your decision?

A: Depends on severity. High impact + unmitigated? Delay launch, fix it properly. Low risk + mitigated? Launch with close monitoring + rollback plan ready. Never ship knowing a critical blocker exists.
